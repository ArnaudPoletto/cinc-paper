Piezoelectric Mattress Sensor Strips for Unobtrusive Cardiac Monitoring: Validation Against ECG Across Sleeping Positions and Mattress Stiffness

Unobtrusive monitoring of cardiac activity during sleep is a key enabler for home-based screening of sleep disorders. Piezoelectric strips arranged equidistantly from head to feet beneath a mattress can capture ballistocardiographic signals without any body-worn device, but their reliability across sleeping positions and mattress stiffness remains insufficiently characterized. Moreover, when multiple strips are available, combining their signals effectively is essential to achieve robust detection.

We present a signal processing pipeline for detecting beat-to-beat cardiac intervals from 16 equidistant piezoelectric strips spanning head to feet, validated against simultaneous ECG as the reference standard. The dataset comprises recordings from 7 healthy participants, each lying in four standardized positions (supine, prone, left, right) for 3 minutes per position on 6 mattresses of varying stiffness, totalling 139 recordings. Cardiac detection uses template matching with dominant shape extraction; signals from all 16 strips are then fused into a single ensemble estimate weighted by per-strip signal quality, quantified from the regularity of detected intervals and peak prominences. Detected intervals are paired with ECG intervals via Hungarian assignment, accepting pairs whose durations differ by less than 30 ms. The matching rate is the fraction of ECG intervals with an accepted pair. Performance is assessed by (i) the proportion of matched intervals per recording, (ii) the residual duration difference of matched beats as a precision metric, and (iii) the effect of mattress and body position on matching performance, evaluated against a pre-specified 5 percentage-point (pp) equivalence margin chosen to reflect a clinically negligible change in interval-detection performance.

Across 139 recordings, ensemble fusion achieves a median interval-matching rate of 97.6% (IQR 92.6%–99.5%), with 15 recordings (10.8%) falling outside the IQR fences. On average, 5.0 out of 16 strips exceed 80% matching per recording, enough to support robust ensemble fusion, but too few to guarantee that any single pre-selected strip would be reliable. The residual duration difference between matched piezoelectric and ECG intervals has a standard deviation of 7.29 ms (n ≈ 25,000 beats); propagated through a successive-difference HRV metric, this corresponds to an inflation of a typical resting RMSSD (≈ 30 ms) of approximately 6%, well within clinical tolerance for downstream HRV analysis. Mattress stiffness and body position have no practically meaningful effect on matching performance: the maximum pairwise difference between any two mattress medians is 4.58 pp, and 3.42 pp across positions, both within the pre-specified 5 pp equivalence margin.

These results demonstrate that piezoelectric mattress sensor strips, combined with template-matching detection and quality-weighted ensemble fusion, deliver cardiac interval estimates that are simultaneously accurate (median matching 97.6%, IQR 92.6%–99.5%), precise (7.29 ms beat-level std), and robust (no practically meaningful effect of mattress or position). This supports their use for long-term, unobtrusive cardiac monitoring in home and clinical environments, with HRV-based stress monitoring as a natural downstream application.
