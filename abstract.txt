Piezoelectric Mattress Sensor Arrays for Cardiac and Respiratory Monitoring: Validation Against Polysomnography

Unobtrusive monitoring of cardiac and respiratory activity during sleep is a key enabler for home-based screening of cardiovascular and respiratory disorders. Piezoelectric sensors embedded in a mattress can capture ballistocardiogram (BCG) and respiratory signals without any body-worn device, but their reliability across sleeping positions and mattress compositions remains insufficiently characterized.

We present a signal processing pipeline for detecting beat-to-beat cardiac intervals and breath-to-breath respiratory intervals from a 16-sensor piezoelectric array, validated against simultaneous polysomnography (electrocardiography (ECG) and respiratory belts) as the reference standard. Controlled recordings were acquired from 8 healthy participants, each lying in four standardized positions (supine, prone, left, right) for 3 minutes per position, on multiple mattress types (N=X configurations). Cardiac detection uses template matching with dominant shape extraction and multi-sensor ensemble fusion weighted by signal quality. Respiratory detection uses adaptive frequency band selection with ensemble combination. Both pipelines include gap filling and interval validation based on robust statistics.

Preliminary results show concordance of approximately X% between piezoelectric-derived and PSG-derived cardiac intervals, and approximately X% for respiratory intervals. Detection performance was consistent across supine, lateral, and prone positions. Notably, no significant degradation was observed across the different mattress types tested, suggesting robustness of the approach to variations in mattress composition and stiffness.

These findings indicate that piezoelectric mattress sensor arrays, combined with template-matching and ensemble signal processing, can provide reliable cardiac and respiratory interval estimates across realistic sleeping conditions. This approach has potential for long-term, unobtrusive cardiorespiratory monitoring in home and clinical environments.
